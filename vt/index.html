Virtual Tour Construction and Constellation Optimization on Smartphones


Virtual tour applications such as Google Streetview have gained their popularity by providing visual navigation hints and immersed user experience. Commonly, a virtual tour consists of both image data and spatial connections, and is represented as a constellation of scenes. The construction of virtual tours originally requires sophisticated devices and time-consuming site survey.

Lately, the technology advance in smartphones facilitate the virtual tour capture on these versatile end devices. However, the existing smartphone-based solutions suffer from the inaccuracy of constellation reconstruction. The constellation data relies on the GPS and compass readings, which can be easily corrupted due to surrounding geomagnetic interference. Our investigation shows that deviations lie in both image orientation and spatial connection (including distance and orientation) when compared with the ground truth results.

In this work, we design and implement vCanvas, a complete solution for virtual tour construction and constellation optimization on smartphones. With the core techniques lying in the optimization-based multi-modal data fusion, we resolved challenges including accurate orientation and distance calibration via taking advantage of both sensor readings and panoramic image correlations.
